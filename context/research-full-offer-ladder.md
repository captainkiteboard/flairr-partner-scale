**Date:** 2026-07-04

## The Insight

The AI Assessment/Audit is the front door. But instead of being a standalone cost, the fee **credits toward the first implementation.** 

The client's internal logic:

> "If I do the assessment and move forward, I paid nothing extra for it. If I don't move forward, I still got a professional AI audit of my business worth way more than what I paid. Either way I win."

**There is no scenario where the client loses.** That's what makes it irresistible.

### Why Everyone Says Yes

The objection to any consulting engagement is: "What if I pay and get nothing useful?"

This offer kills that:
- **If you proceed:** the assessment was free — it credited to your build
- **If you don't:** you still have a professional AI audit with specific ROI projections you can act on later or give to another vendor
- **The audit firm recommended it:** built-in trust
- **It's $2,500:** low enough to be a no-brainer for any SME doing $500K+

### How It Sells (The Partner's Pitch)

The audit/bookkeeping firm says this during a normal client meeting:

> "We've been looking at your processes as part of our usual work, and there are some clear areas where AI could save you serious time and money. We offer an AI Readiness Assessment — it maps everything out and shows you exactly where the ROI is.

**That's a 30-second pitch. No technical knowledge needed.** The partner doesn't sell AI — they sell clarity.

## The Conversion Psychology

### Why The Credit Mechanic Is Genius

**1. It reframes the assessment from "cost" to "deposit"**
Clients don't think "I'm spending $2,500 on a report." They think "I'm putting $2,500 toward my AI build, and I get a free assessment as part of it."

**2. It creates forward momentum**
Once they've paid $2,500 and received a report showing $50K+ in potential savings, the psychology shifts. They've already invested. The numbers are in front of them. Not moving forward feels like leaving money on the table.

**3. It eliminates buyer's remorse on the assessment**
Even if they don't proceed, they can't feel ripped off — $2,500 for a professional AI audit with specific ROI numbers is objectively valuable. They'd pay a consultant $5K-$10K for the same thing.

**4. It makes the implementation feel cheaper**
A $12,500 AIOS install feels like $10,000 because they already "paid" $2,500 of it. The perceived price dropped 20% without Flairr discounting anything.

**5. It funds Flairr's sales process**
Flairr gets $1,625 per assessment. Even if only 50% of assessments convert, the other 50% covered their cost. The assessment **pays for its own pipeline.** This is client-financed acquisition.

## How Flairr Delivers The Assessment (Templated)

### The 5-Day Assessment Process

| Day | Activity | Who |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1** | Kickoff call. Collect: org chart, tool list, key processes, pain points. Partner joins call. | Flairr + Partner + Client |
| **Day 2** | Process mapping. Walk through top 5-8 workflows. Screen share or Loom recordings from client. | Flairr + Client |
| **Day 3** | Analysis. Score each process for automation potential. Calculate ROI projections. | Flairr (internal) |
| **Day 4** | Build report. Executive summary, process map, opportunity scores, top 3 quick wins, 90-day roadmap. | Flairr (internal) |
| **Day 5** | Debrief presentation. Walk through findings. Partner co-presents. Close on implementation. | Flairr + Partner + Client |

### Flairr's COGS Per Assessment

| Cost | Amount |
|---|---|
| Senior consultant time (8-10 hours) | $800-$1,000 |
| Report template + customization (2 hours) | $200 |
| Tools/infrastructure | $50 |
| **Total COGS** | **~$1,050-$1,250** |
| **Revenue** | **$2,500** |
| **Gross margin** | **~50-58%** |
