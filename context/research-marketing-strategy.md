# Marketing Strategy — Hormozi Playbook Applied to Flairr's Partner Program

**Date:** 2026-07-04

---

## The Marketing Problem (Clearly Stated)

Flairr has two customers to acquire, not one:

1. **Partners** (audit/bookkeeping firms) — who sell to their clients on Flairr's behalf
2. **End clients** (SMEs) — who the partners bring, but Flairr needs to help close

The marketing strategy must recruit partners AND arm them to sell. Two audiences, one system.

---

## Hormozi's Core Four — Applied To Flairr

Hormozi says there are only four ways to generate leads:

| Method | What It Is | Flairr's Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm Outreach** | 1-to-1 with people who know you | Partner recruitment (existing network) |
| **Content** | 1-to-many with warm audience | Authority building (LinkedIn, speaking) |
| **Cold Outreach** | 1-to-1 with strangers | Partner recruitment at scale |
| **Paid Ads** | 1-to-many with cold audience | End-client awareness (later phase) |

**Hormozi's rule: Start with Warm Outreach + Content. Add Cold Outreach. Then Paid Ads once validated.**

But here's the twist: **Flairr doesn't need to market to end clients at all.** The partners do that. Flairr only needs to market to partners. That changes everything.

---

## The Marketing Angle

### You're Not Selling AI. You're Selling Revenue.

**To partners (audit/bookkeeping firms):**

> "Your clients are asking about AI. Right now you're saying 'we're looking into it.' Meanwhile, firms offering AI advisory earn 30% more MRR than compliance-only firms. We give you the AI advisory service to sell — you keep 35% of every deal. No hiring. No learning curve. Your clients get AI that works. You get a new revenue stream."

**The partner doesn't care about AI technology.** They care about:
- Revenue they're leaving on the table
- Clients they're about to lose to someone who DOES offer AI services
- Advisory income that doesn't require more accountants

**To end clients (via the partner):**

> "Your accountant identified $X in potential savings through AI automation. The assessment is $2,500, and it credits 100% toward any implementation. You keep the full report either way."

**The end client doesn't care about AI either.** They care about:
- The money they're losing
- The hours their team is wasting
- The fact that their trusted accountant recommended it

### The Core Message To Partners (One Line)

> **"Turn your client relationships into AI advisory revenue — without hiring AI talent."**

That's the entire marketing angle. Everything else supports this.

## Phase 2: Content (Ongoing From Week 1) — Build Authority

### Platform: LinkedIn (Primary)

LinkedIn is where accounting firm partners, auditors, and governance professionals live. One platform. Go deep.

### Content Strategy: The "Give Away The Playbook" Approach

Hormozi's rule: 90% value, 10% promotion. **Give away so much insight that the partner thinks "if they're sharing THIS for free, what do they deliver when we pay?"**

### Content Pillars (4 Rotating Themes)

**Pillar 1: "Your Clients Are Bleeding Money" (Problem Awareness)**
Posts that surface the pain accounting firms see in their clients but don't know how to fix.

Example posts:
- "Your client spent $8K this month on admin staff doing data entry. Here's what that number looks like over 5 years."
- "A firm with 500 tax clients processes 5,000 documents. Here's why it still takes 3 months."
- "Board packs have grown to 226 pages. Only 48% add value. The rest is noise."

**Pillar 2: "Here's Exactly How We Fixed It" (Proof)**
Case studies with real numbers. Show the result. Don't hide the method.

Example posts:
- "We installed a document collection system for an accounting client. 215% more documents. 142 hours/week saved. Here's the before and after."
- "An AI support system with 858% ROI. What it costs, what it does, what changed."
- "Board pack assembly went from 4 weeks to 2 days. Here's how."

**Pillar 3: "The Accounting Advisory Shift" (Industry Trend)**
Position Flairr as understanding the accountant's world, not just selling tech.

Example posts:
- "94% of accounting firms say advisory revenue is key. But advisory of WHAT? Here's the highest-ROI advisory service nobody's offering yet."
- "Compliance is being commoditized. The firms winning in 2026 are selling oversight, not outputs."
- "Your audit already surfaces AI opportunities. You're just not charging for it."

**Pillar 4: "The Partner Model" (Soft Sell)**
Show what partnership looks like without hard pitching.

Example posts:
- "An audit firm added $165K in advisory revenue last year without hiring anyone. Here's the model."
- "We don't sell to your clients. We help YOU sell to your clients. Here's the difference."
- "Why accounting firms are the perfect AI distribution channel (and nobody's figured it out yet)."

### Content Calendar

| Day | Content Type | Pillar |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Short insight post (3-5 lines) | Problem Awareness |
| Tuesday | Case study or result (with numbers) | Proof |
| Wednesday | Industry trend / hot take | Advisory Shift |
| Thursday | Longer post (the kind you already write — stacked observations, contrast) | Rotating |
| Friday | Soft partner story or behind-the-scenes | Partner Model |

### Content Format (In Your Voice)

Your LinkedIn post about board packs is the template. The voice is already there:
- Short sentences. Punchy.
- Stack observations. Build the argument.
- Use contrast to land the point.
- End with a takeaway or CTA.
- No corporate speak. No fluff.

**Do NOT change the voice.** It works. Just point it at the partner-recruitment angle.

### Lead Magnet

**"The AI Advisory Playbook for Accounting Firms"**

A free downloadable PDF (or a LinkedIn article series) that gives away the entire framework:
- How to spot AI opportunities in your client base (the 10 things to listen for)
- The assessment process (what to ask, how to score)
- ROI benchmarks by use case (document automation, support, onboarding)
- The partner model (how to sell it, what you keep)
- Case studies with real numbers

**Why give it all away?** Hormozi's principle: "The more you give, the more they trust. The more they trust, the more they buy." An accounting firm that reads this playbook and thinks "we could do this ourselves" will quickly realize they can't build the AI systems. They need Flairr. The playbook sells the partnership.

**CTA on the lead magnet:** "Want to add AI advisory services to your firm? Book a 20-minute partner call."

## Phase 4: Paid Ads (Month 3+) — After Validation

### When To Turn On Ads

Only after:
- 5+ partners signed and active
- Assessment → implementation conversion rate confirmed (target: 50%+)
- At least 2 case studies with hard ROI numbers
- Content is generating inbound interest

### Two Ad Campaigns

**Campaign 1: Partner Recruitment (LinkedIn Ads)**

Target: Managing partners / directors at accounting firms, 10-200 employees
Ad format: Single image or short video
Hook: "Accounting firms are adding $100K+ in AI advisory revenue. Without hiring AI talent."
CTA: Download the AI Advisory Playbook → book a partner call

Budget: $2,000-$5,000/month
Expected CAC per partner: $500-$2,000
Lifetime value per partner: $30K-$150K/year → wildly profitable

**Campaign 2: End-Client Awareness (Facebook/LinkedIn — support the partner)**

This is NOT Flairr acquiring clients directly. This is **air cover for the partner.**

When a partner brings up AI advisory to a client, and that client googles "AI automation for accounting" or "AI readiness assessment" — Flairr (or co-branded with the partner) should show up.

Target: Business owners, CFOs, operations managers in partner's region
Ad format: Case study video or carousel
Hook: "We saved an accounting firm's client 142 hours per week. Here's how."
CTA: "Ask your accountant about an AI Readiness Assessment"

This drives end-clients to ask their accountant — which is the partner's sales trigger.

Budget: $1,000-$3,000/month
This is support spending, not direct acquisition.

## Phase 5: Leverage Other People (Ongoing) — Hormozi's "Lead Getters"

### The Four Lead Getters Applied To Flairr

| Lead Getter | Hormozi Definition | Flairr Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Customers** | Referrals from happy clients | Partner firms referring other firms. End-clients referring businesses to their accountant's AI service. |
| **Employees** | Team members who generate leads | Flairr's delivery team flags upsell opportunities during AIOS installs. Partners' staff spot AI opportunities in client meetings. |
| **Affiliates** | People with audiences who promote you | Accounting influencers, governance consultants, Xero/QBO community leaders. Pay per partner referral. |
| **Agencies** | Firms that do marketing for you | Only needed at scale. Not Phase 1. |

### The Referral Loop

```
Partner sells to Client A
    → Client A gets results
    → Client A tells business friends
    → Friends ask THEIR accountant about AI
    → That accountant becomes a new partner
    → New partner sells to their clients
    → Cycle repeats
```

**This is the Gym Launch flywheel.** Gym owners told other gym owners. The product sold the product.

### Affiliate Play: Accounting Influencers

| Type | Example | Deal |
|---|---|---|
| Accounting YouTubers/podcasters | Channels covering firm growth, advisory, tech | Sponsored segment or interview. $500-$2K per appearance + affiliate fee per partner signed. |
| Accounting association speakers | SAICA, NBA, AICPA chapter events | Speak on "AI Advisory Revenue for Firms." Free. The talk IS the marketing. |
| Governance consultants | People who advise boards | Referral fee for every Board Pack Intelligence client they introduce. |
| Xero/QBO community leaders | Regional partner network organizers | Co-host a webinar: "Adding AI to your advisory stack." |
